👩‍💼 Generation X (Born ~1965–1980)

Language shaped by cynicism, early tech, and independence

  • ✅ “Ironic”
    🔍 Real meaning: A contradiction between expectation and reality
    📍 Misused as "unfortunate."
  • ✅ “Ambivalent”
    🔍 Meant: Mixed feelings
    📍 Often confused with "indifferent."
  • ✅ “Literally”
    🔍 Actually true
    📍 Now also used for emphasis or exaggeration.
  • ✅ “Whatever”
    🔍 Popular Gen X phrase of apathy or rebellion
    📍 Now iconic in sarcasm.
  • ✅ “Radical”
    🔍 80s slang meaning awesome or cool
    📍 Now sounds retro or ironic.
  • ✅ “Sell out”
    🔍 Someone who sacrifices values for money or fame
    📍 Term of cultural critique.
  • ✅ “Bodacious”
    🔍 Slang for bold or excellent
    📍 Popularized by surfer/skater culture.
  • ✅ “Hack”
    🔍 Originally a tech term for clever coding
    📍 Now also means life shortcut or DIY trick.
  • ✅ “Yuppie”
    🔍 Young Urban Professional
    📍 Symbol of 80s ambition and consumerism.
  • ✅ “Grunge”
    🔍 Describes a music/fashion subculture
    📍 Now used more broadly to describe a rough or unpolished look.
