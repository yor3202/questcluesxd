🧑‍💻 Millennials (Born ~1981–1996)

Language shaped by the internet, memes, and sarcasm

  • ✅ “Adulting”
    🔍 Means: Taking on responsibilities
    📍 Often misunderstood by older generations as being lazy or sarcastic
  • ✅ “Toxic” (personality)
    🔍 Not just chemical—it refers to harmful behavior in relationships
    📍 Now a psychological or social buzzword
  • ✅ “Triggered”
    🔍 Originally a trauma-related term
    📍 Often misunderstood as being “too sensitive” or a joke—context matters deeply
  • ✅ “Ghosting”
    🔍 Disappearing suddenly from communication, especially in dating
    📍 Became widespread with texting and dating apps
  • ✅ “FOMO”
    🔍 Fear Of Missing Out
    📍 Fueled by social media comparison culture
  • ✅ “Woke”
    🔍 Originally meant being socially aware
    📍 Now often politicized or used sarcastically
  • ✅ “Savage”
    🔍 Used to describe someone brutally honest or bold in a funny way
    📍 Popularized through meme culture
  • ✅ “Cancel” / “Cancelled”
    🔍 Cutting off support for someone after problematic behavior
    📍 Rooted in social media activism, now highly debated
  • ✅ “Lit”
    🔍 Means something exciting or awesome
    📍 Adopted into mainstream from hip-hop slang
  • ✅ “Flex”
    🔍 Showing off, sometimes subtly
    📍 Can be sincere or ironic depending on context
