🧒 Gen Alpha (Born ~2013–Today)

Still forming language identity, but already using slang from YouTube, gaming, and Gen Z.

“Let’s gooo!”

🎉 Emphasized excitement or victory

🏁 Common in gaming and YouTube content for celebrating wins or good news

“Sus”

🕵️‍♂️ Short for “suspicious”

🎮 Popularized by the game *Among Us* to describe someone acting strangely

💀 (Skull Emoji)

🤣 Used to represent “I’m dead,” meaning something is extremely funny

📱 Commonly used in reaction to jokes or memes

“Triggered”

🔍 Originally a trauma-related term

📍 Often misunderstood as being “too sensitive” or a joke—context matters deeply

“Cap / No Cap”

🧢 “Cap” means a lie, “No cap” means being honest or for real

✔️ “No cap, that was amazing” = “Seriously, that was amazing”

“Rizz”

💬 Short for charisma; ability to attract someone romantically

❤️ “He’s got rizz” = “He’s smooth or charming”

“Slay”

🔥 Means someone is doing really well or looks amazing

🎤 “You slayed that performance!” = “You did great!”

“Mid”

⚖️ Short for “mediocre” or “average”

👎 “That movie was mid” = “It was just okay, nothing special”

“NPC”

🎮 Stands for “Non-Playable Character” in games

🧍‍♂️ Used to describe someone acting robotic, predictable, or not self-aware

“Ratio”

📊 Used on social media when replies or dislikes outnumber likes—shows disapproval

💬 “You got ratioed” = Your comment was unpopular

“Sigma”

🧠 Refers to someone who is independent, confident, and doesn’t follow crowds

💪 “That’s so sigma of you” = Acting cool or unaffected by pressure

“W / L”

🏆 “W” = Win, “L” = Loss

🗣️ “That’s a W” = “That’s a win” / “You took an L” = “You lost or failed”

“Caught in 4K”

📷 Means caught doing something wrong or embarrassing with proof (like a high-res video)

🔍 Often used with screenshots or video memes
