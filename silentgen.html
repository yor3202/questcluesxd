👴 Silent Generation (Born ~1928–1945)
Language shaped by war, tradition, and formality

“Gay”

✅ Common Use Then: “He’s in a gay mood today.”

🔍 Meant: Happy or carefree

📍 Now mostly associated with sexual orientation

“Swell”

✅ Common Use: “That’s a swell idea!”

🔍 Meant: Excellent or very good

📍 Now sounds old-fashioned or sarcastic

“Negotiate”

✅ Used in: Post-WWII diplomacy or work

🔍 Meant: To navigate or handle situations

📍 Now often only seen as formal or business-like

“Howdy”

✅ Common Use: “Howdy, partner!”

🔍 Meant: Friendly hello

📍 Now seen as Western or old-fashioned

“Gee whiz”

✅ Used for: Surprise or amazement

🔍 Meant: A mild exclamation

📍 Rarely used today, feels nostalgic

“Dungarees”

✅ Meant: Blue jeans or work pants

🔍 Common term for trousers in the era

📍 Now replaced by “jeans”

“Court”

✅ Used as: “He’s courting her.”

🔍 Meant: Dating seriously with intent to marry

📍 Now replaced by “dating”

“Malarkey”

✅ Meant: Nonsense or foolish talk

🔍 Popular in political or tough talk

📍 Rarely used in modern conversation

“Whippersnapper”

✅ Meant: An impudent or young person

🔍 Used by elders to talk about youth

📍 Now mostly a joke or cliché

“Golly”

✅ Used as: “Golly, that’s amazing!”

🔍 Meant: Expression of surprise

📍 Now rarely heard, considered dated
