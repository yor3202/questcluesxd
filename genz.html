👩‍🎤 Gen Z (Born ~1997–2012)

Language shaped by TikTok, meme culture, and speed

  • “Slay”
    ✅ Used in: “You slayed that outfit.”
    🔍 Meant: Did something very well or with confidence
    📍 Confusing without cultural context
  • “Ghosted”
    ✅ Used in: Dating or friendship
    🔍 Means: Suddenly cut off all communication
    📍 Older generations may not grasp the emotional impact it implies today
  • “Cap / No Cap”
    🔍 Cap = Lie; No cap = Truth
    📍 Without context, sounds confusing: “That’s cap.” → “You’re lying.”
  • “It’s giving ___”
    ✅ Used to describe a vibe
    🔍 “It’s giving boss energy.”
    📍 May seem grammatically odd to older generations, but it’s all about the vibe match
  • “Bet”
    🔍 Means: Agreement or approval
    📍 Used as a quick affirmative like “OK” or “Sure”
  • “Ratio”
    🔍 On social media: More replies than likes = negative response
    📍 Originated on Twitter, often used to call out bad takes
  • “Sus”
    🔍 Short for "suspicious"
    📍 Popularized by the game *Among Us*
  • “Main character energy”
    🔍 Acting like you’re the star of the show
    📍 Embracing confidence or attention in a narrative-like way
  • “Bussin’”
    🔍 Means: Really good (especially food)
    📍 “This pizza is bussin’!” = extremely tasty
  • “Pushin P”
    🔍 Origin: Rap culture, esp. Gunna
    📍 Means: Keeping it real, showing positivity or success
